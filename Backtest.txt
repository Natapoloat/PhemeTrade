  Honest verdict

  Three things to hold in mind together:

  1. This is the in-sample number. The out-of-sample walk-forward on this exact filter came in at −0.04 R / PF 0.67–0.89 (per the validation log). So +0.115
  R is the optimistic end; the honest expectation is ~breakeven-negative. The FVG filter was the first change in the project to move OOS the right way at
  all — real, but not sufficient.
  2. Even taking +0.115 R at face value, it's untradeable as an economic strategy. 3.6% over 21.6 years, CAGR 0.16%, and a ~10.6-year max drawdown duration.
  Three trades a year at 0.5% risk cannot compound into anything, and no human sits through a decade underwater on a 3.5% edge.
  3. Two identifiable leaks are still in the config: the CHoCH exit is net-negative (−0.05 R × 16), and stops realize −1.09 R vs −1.0 R planned. Turning off
  use_choch_exit is a cheap, honest ablation worth checking — but it won't change the fundamental verdict.

  Bottom line: the number is genuinely positive in-sample and the FVG filter is the one real improvement found, but the strategy has no deployable edge —
  the OOS is ~breakeven and the economics (frequency, CAGR, DD duration, single-sided concentration) are non-viable. This is exactly why the current phase
  is forward-testing to gather honest OOS data, not going live.
